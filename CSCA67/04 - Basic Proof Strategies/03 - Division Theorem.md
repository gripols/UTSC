Let $m$ and $n$ be natural numbers ($m, n \in \mathbb{N}$, $n \neq 0$). Then, there exist unique integers $Q$ (quotient) and $R$ (remainder) such that
$m = n Q + R \quad \text{with} \quad 0 \le R < n.$
